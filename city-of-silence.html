About This Book

In The City of Silence, Asa enters a city where nothing speaks in the way he expects. The ancient halls do not answer with words, signs, or commands. Instead, they listen.

As the silence deepens around him, Asa begins to understand that this city is not empty. It is waiting for stillness, for trust, and for the kind of recognition that cannot be forced. With Saxifraga near him and the quiet hum of life beginning to return, Asa must learn that silence is not the absence of meaning. It is the place where meaning first begins.
